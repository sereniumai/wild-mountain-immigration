## 6. TECHNICAL SEO & SCHEMA (Next.js)

**Stack:** Next.js App Router (v15/16) + Tailwind + TypeScript.

**Schema (JSON-LD, `@graph` in root layout, cross-referenced by `@id`):**
- **`["Organization","ProfessionalService"]`** site-wide (NOT `LegalService`/`Attorney` — inaccurate for an RCIC). priceRange `"$$"`, areaServed = Canada + Alberta + cities, `knowsAbout` = program topics.
- **`Person` (Nicola)** with `hasCredential` → `EducationalOccupationalCredential` (RCIC, `recognizedBy` CICC, `identifier` R706497) + `sameAs` → CICC public register + LinkedIn. **This is the strongest E-E-A-T signal we can ship** and what AI cites for regulated professionals.
- **`Service`** per service page; **`FAQPage`** + **`HowTo`** (see note); **`BreadcrumbList`** every nested page; **`Article`/`BlogPosting`** with `author` → Nicola; **`WebSite`+`SearchAction`** (only if a real `/search` route ships).

**⚠️ 2026 schema reality:** **FAQ rich results were removed from Google Search May 7 2026; HowTo rich results retired 2023.** **Keep the schema anyway** — it's now an **AI-citation play** (FAQPage-marked pages are ~3.2× more likely to appear in AI Overviews) + valid Schema.org, zero penalty. The "competitors have no FAQ schema" gap is still real — just reframed as AI share, not SERP stars.

**Review/AggregateRating:** **Do NOT self-host firm AggregateRating for SERP stars** — Google won't show self-serving stars + 2026 manual-action risk + CICC s.45. Display consent-approved testimonials as visible content (no schema); drive review *acquisition* to GBP (those show as third-party stars safely).

**Core Web Vitals 2026 (75th-pct mobile):** LCP ≤2.5s, **INP ≤200ms** (the priority — ~43% of sites fail), CLS ≤0.1.
- LCP: `next/image`, preload the **single** hero only (Next 16: `preload`, not deprecated `priority`); AVIF/WebP.
- Fonts: `next/font` (self-host, swap, size-adjust), 1–2 families.
- INP: **RSC by default**, `"use client"` only at interactive leaves (island architecture ≈ −35% client JS); defer chat/analytics via `next/script` `lazyOnload`.
- Bundle: `@next/bundle-analyzer`, `optimizePackageImports`, no moment.js.

**Rendering:** Default **SSG** (home, services, guides). **ISR** for blog (`revalidate=86400`) and **draw-results pages** (short window or **on-demand `revalidateTag`** when a new draw publishes — keeps a stable, indexable, fresh URL). SSR only for personalized pages, which get `noindex`.

**Crawl/index:** `app/sitemap.ts` (accurate `lastModified`), `app/robots.ts`, **manual canonicals** via Metadata API (Next doesn't auto-add them), real crawlable pagination, hreflang only if a `fr-CA` version ships.

**The 117 deleted legacy URLs:** **301** (in `next.config.js redirects()`) any with a modern equivalent (most); **410** (via `middleware.ts` returning status 410) the dead-no-equivalent ones. Keep redirects ≤1 hop. `noindex` search/tag/thank-you pages to avoid index bloat.

**Metadata:** `metadataBase` + title template in root layout; `generateMetadata` for dynamic pages; dynamic OG images via `opengraph-image.tsx` (`next/og`, inline styles only) with Nicola's photo + RCIC badge. Title ≤60 chars, description ≤155, **no guarantee language**.

**Monitor:** GSC (index coverage for the legacy-URL transition + CWV report), Schema.org Validator + Rich Results Test, PageSpeed Insights + `web-vitals` RUM (essential for INP), periodic Screaming Frog crawl (canonicals, redirect hops, orphans).

## 7. AI SEARCH (AEO/GEO) — get cited by ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, AI Overviews

**Why this matters most in our vertical:** ~41% of people now start legal/immigration searches with an AI assistant, and **immigration is the highest AI-Overview-trigger vertical**. YMYL accuracy gates are strict — which favors a credentialed, statute-accurate, fresh RCIC over generic competitors.

**What makes content citable (and the measured lift):** answer-first (44% of citations come from the first 30% of a page); **statistics with dates** (+30–41%); **citations to authoritative sources** (+30%); **named expert quotes** (+30–41%); freshness (AI-cited content is ~26% fresher; citations decay after ~3 months); entity clarity.

**Formatting every key page:**
- **Answer the heading question in the first 40–60 words** (self-contained, extractable), then expand.
- **Question-based H2/H3** mirroring real queries ("How long does AAIP take in 2026?").
- **Fact tables** (CRS cutoffs, processing times, fees, eligibility) — AI extracts tables extremely well.
- **TL;DR / key-takeaways box** at top of long guides; real **Q&A block** at the bottom (+ FAQPage schema).

**Entity / knowledge graph (durable advantage):** consistent NAP + `sameAs` everywhere (LinkedIn, GBP, **CICC register**); create a **Wikidata item** for Nicola (RCIC) — feeds Google KG + heavily used by ChatGPT/Gemini; attach `author`→Nicola on every article.

**Target query patterns AI Overviews fire on:** "how long…", "how much…", "what is…", "can I…", "do I need…", "X vs Y", "best… for…". **Lean factual/procedural** (processing times, scores, eligibility) — Google suppresses AIOs on advice-style "should I…" YMYL queries.

**AI crawlers:** In robots.txt **allow** GPTBot, OAI-SearchBot, Google-Extended, PerplexityBot, ClaudeBot (we want citations). Optionally block pure scrapers (Bytespider, CCBot). Ship a lightweight **llms.txt** indexing cornerstone guides (secondary aid; robots.txt is primary).

**Measure:** pick a fixed set of ~30–50 real immigration prompts; track citation share monthly across engines (Otterly.ai / LLMrefs, or manual baseline); watch GA4 referrals from chat.openai.com / perplexity.ai / gemini.

**Synergy:** AEO is built on SEO — the same E-E-A-T signals Google trusts for YMYL are exactly what LLMs cite. Nicola's RCIC credential is the #1 asset for both.

## 9. CICC COMPLIANCE — BINDING ON ALL COPY (Code SOR/2022-128, s.44–45)

**Mandatory (s.44(1)):** registered name prominent; **CICC public-register URL on every written ad**; **RCIC #R706497** displayed.

**Prohibited (s.44(2)) — copy checklist:**
- ❌ No guaranteeing success of any application/EOI/proceeding ("guaranteed PR", "100% approval").
- ❌ No false/misleading claims (no unsubstantiated "best/#1", no overstated success rates).
- ❌ No implying any relationship with IRCC / Government of Canada / a province (no govt logos; cite IRCC as a *source*, not an affiliation).

**Copy swaps:** "guaranteed approval" → "the strongest possible application"; "we'll get you PR" → "we guide you through every step"; "approved by IRCC" → show CICC licence + cite IRCC as source. Standing disclaimer near CTAs/case studies: *"Outcomes depend on individual circumstances and IRCC/AAIP decisions. We do not guarantee results. Nicola Wightman, RCIC #R706497 — verify at [CICC register]."*

**Testimonials (s.45):** only genuine, accurate, and **approved in writing by the client** — keep consent on file for every testimonial/case study (incl. any given to press). **2026 brought tougher consultant rules + broadened Competition Act misleading-ad exposure** — substantiate every claim.
