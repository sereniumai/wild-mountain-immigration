# Multi-Channel Strategy — Video, Community, Programmatic SEO (2026)

**Context:** Solo RCIC, Cochrane AB, standard RCIC class. Two hard constraints govern everything: (1) **CICC Code (SOR/2022-128)** — name/RCIC# at/near the start of any ad, no outcome guarantees, no public file-specific advice; (2) **2026 algorithm reality** — Google's scaled-content-abuse policy deindexes thin templated pages, while **YouTube and Reddit are now the #1 and #2 most-cited domains in AI Overviews.** Lean into the two AI-favored channels; be conservative on the penalty-prone one.

---

## PART 1 — VIDEO / YOUTUBE SEO

**Why it matters:** YouTube is cited in up to ~29.5% of Google AI Overviews (top domain); YouTube AIO citations surged 414%. ~70% of YT watch time is from recommendations (long-tail evergreen value). For YMYL, a real RCIC's face on camera is the highest-trust signal you can produce.

**Topics (priority):**
- **Tier 1 (evergreen + commercial intent — build first):** CRS explainers ("How the CRS works 2026", "CRS 470 — your real options"); **AAIP walkthroughs** (your local moat — few big channels cover Alberta streams in depth); occupation eligibility ("Can a [welder/nurse/truck driver] move to Canada?" → maps to occupation pages).
- **Tier 2 (topical — subscribers + recency):** Express Entry draw recaps (fast turnaround after each IRCC draw); policy-change reactions (keep general, not file-specific).
- **Tier 3 (top-of-funnel reach):** "Day in the life / settlement in Cochrane/Calgary, cost of living, winter, jobs"; client-Q&A/FAQ roundups (answer-style = AI-extractable).

**Mechanics:** front-load keyword in titles (no stuffing); first 2–3 description lines carry keyword + value prop, then 150–300 word summary + **timestamped chapters** + money-page link + CICC compliance block; **add chapters to every video** (Google surfaces Key Moments + AI Overviews deep-link to timestamps — highest-leverage low-effort tactic); upload accurate **transcripts** (the text layer AI/Google parse); high-contrast thumbnails (≤4 words + face + hook); strong 5–10s hook for retention.

**Money-page play:** embed each video on its matching money page — boosts dwell time (a top 2026 quality signal) and double-dips distribution. **Short-form (Shorts/Reels/TikTok):** top-of-funnel reach only; pick ONE primary platform (Reels + YT Shorts strongest for a local RCIC), slice long videos into 30–60s clips; don't post watermarked TikTok exports to Reels.

**Cadence (solo):** 1 long-form/week (or biweekly if quality slips), 3–5 Shorts/week batched (mostly clips of long-form). Film 2–4 long videos per monthly session. Prioritize the AAIP/CRS evergreen library over chasing every draw.

**CICC for video (non-negotiable):** name + RCIC# announced/shown at/near the START of every video + in description/pinned comment; no outcome guarantees; no file-specific advice in videos or comments; standing disclaimer "general information, not legal advice — book a consultation"; no implied govt affiliation.

**Model channels:** Canadian Immigration Channel (authority framing + breadth); Mark Holthe (single trusted-expert-face model — like a solo RCIC); large news/update channels (draw-recap cadence builds subs); "Express Entry journey"/settlement channels (humanize + reach). Audit Feedspot's "80 Canada Immigration YouTubers" — most under-serve **AAIP/Alberta** = your opening.

## PART 2 — REDDIT / COMMUNITY / FORUMS

**Why top-3 priority:** Reddit is the #1–2 most-cited source across AI engines (~68% of AI responses); Reddit AIO citations grew ~450% in 3 months; Google now pulls community perspectives/quotes from Reddit & forums into AI Overviews. As valuable as backlinks once were — **but cannot be faked** (campaign spikes collapse when inauthentic activity stops).

**Where (named):** r/ImmigrationCanada (core), r/PR_Canada, r/ExpressEntry, **r/Calgary + r/Alberta** (geo moat — AAIP + "moving to Alberta"), r/Canada/r/immigration (selective), CanadaVisa forum (read its self-promo rules first), Quora ("is X consultant legit" — entity-building under your real name), expat Facebook groups ("Newcomers to Calgary/Alberta", "[Country]ans in Canada").

**How citations work (format for it):** AI lifts detailed first-hand advice — answer context→specifics→trade-offs as a short numbered list, real numbers/timelines/named programs (AAIP stream names, NOC codes, processing times); build a consistent helpful stance across many related threads (clustering reads as common wisdom).

**Playbook:** comment-first not post-first (~10–15 genuinely helpful comments/week on high-intent threads); earn karma then disclose honestly ("I'm an RCIC in Alberta, so take this with that context — here's the general rule…" — more credible + compliant than hiding it; pursue verified-professional flair); no spam patterns/sockpuppets/repeated links; respect each community's self-promo ratio (~9:1); mine recurring questions + exact phrasing for YouTube titles, blog FAQs, programmatic H2s.

**CICC limits:** NO specific file-level advice publicly — stay general/educational ("here's how the AAIP EE stream generally works", never "based on your facts, do X"); disclose RCIC status honestly (no astroturfing); no outcome guarantees; referral-fee rules tightening (regs in force ~July 15 2026 — no pay-for-referral with non-licensees). **Channel goal = entity signals + AI citations + referral traffic, NOT closing leads in-thread** (the site + booking page convert).

## PART 3 — PROGRAMMATIC / SCALED SEO (~225 pages)

**Straight answer: proceed, but carefully and in waves.** Programmatic SEO is viable but **high-risk for YMYL in 2026** — Google's scaled-content-abuse policy targets "many pages where content is only slightly different"; "if your only unique element is a city name, you're creating doorway pages" → overnight mass deindexing. **Don't launch all 225 at once.** Build in waves, prove quality + indexation on a small batch, expand only what earns impressions. **80 genuinely useful pages > 225 thin ones.**

**Non-negotiable rule:** every page needs unique, verifiable, page-specific value beyond a swapped keyword — aim ~75% template / 25% genuinely-unique real data (not spun paragraphs).

**Page type 1 — "Move to Canada from {country}" (~20+):** rank well IF genuinely country-specific. Required unique data: country-specific pathways (which streams that nationality uses), credential/ECA specifics (named designated bodies e.g. WES, IELTS/CELPIP/TEF realities, police-cert quirks), visa-office/processing context, real demographic data (that diaspora's size in Alberta, common occupations, cost-of-living delta), country-specific FAQ mined from Reddit/Quora/CanadaVisa, a genuine Cochrane/Alberta hook, one embedded video. **Avoid** identical body text with "[Country]" find-replaced (textbook doorway). If you can't write 300+ words of truly country-specific substance, don't publish yet.

**Page type 2 — Occupation / NOC pages (LOWEST risk, START HERE):** data is inherently unique per occupation. Include: NOC code + TEER, which programs/streams qualify (EE category draws, AAIP occupation eligibility), Alberta labour data (demand, wage ranges from Job Bank, AAIP in-demand status), licensing/credentialing for regulated occupations (nurses→CARNA, trades→Alberta apprenticeship/Red Seal), occupation-specific CRS/pathway notes, FAQ + internal links to {country} pages + matching YouTube explainer.

**Page type 3 — City / service-area pages (HIGHEST doorway risk — most conservative):** strong genuine pages for **Cochrane + Calgary** (real service areas); surrounding towns (Airdrie, Canmore, Banff) only where you can add real local substance (employers, commuting/settlement context, diaspora). **Don't generate a boilerplate page for every Alberta town** — quality coverage of a few real service areas beats thin coverage of fifty.

**Internal linking:** programmatic hub-and-spoke — country ↔ occupation ↔ AAIP/CRS pillar ↔ YouTube embeds; automate contextual cross-links from a relationship map (e.g. "Philippines"→nursing/caregiver occupation pages; "welder"→AAIP trades + top source countries); every page links up to a pillar + across to 3–5 siblings (distinguishes a real resource from a doorway farm).

**Risk balance & rollout:** every page shows RCIC author + name + RCIC# + last-updated + official IRCC/AAIP citations (compliance + ranking necessity for YMYL); no guarantees in templates + standard disclaimer; **Wave 1 = ~15 occupation pages (lowest risk) + Cochrane/Calgary; Wave 2 = top 8–10 source countries by actual lead data**; monitor indexation/impressions in GSC, expand only what earns traffic, kill/merge impressions-zero pages; **human review gate on every page before publish** (Google flags mass-published unreviewed AI content — use AI to draft/enrich, never to mass-publish unread).

**Bottom line:** occupation pages first (safe, data-rich), then carefully differentiated country pages, then a SMALL set of genuinely-local city pages. Stay well under the 225 ceiling until quality + indexation are proven.
