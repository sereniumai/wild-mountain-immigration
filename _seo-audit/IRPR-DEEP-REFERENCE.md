## 2. Practice-critical sections — detail

### 2.1 Definitions (s.1–2)

| Term | Section | Operative definition |
|---|---|---|
| **Common-law partner** | 1(1) | An individual cohabiting with the person **in a conjugal relationship, having so cohabited for at least one year**. |
| Common-law partner (special) | 1(2) | Treated as cohabiting where conjugal relationship ≥1 year but cohabitation impossible due to **persecution or any form of penal control**. |
| **Family member** | 1(3) | (a) the spouse or common-law partner; (b) a **dependent child** of the person or of their spouse/CLP; (c) a dependent child of a dependent child. |
| **Dependent child** | 2 | Child of a parent who is (a) the **biological child** (not adopted by a person other than the spouse/CLP) or **adopted child**, **and** (b) is in one of: **(i) under 22 years of age and not a spouse or common-law partner**, or **(ii) 22 or older who has depended substantially on the financial support of the parent since before age 22 and is unable to be financially self-supporting due to a physical or mental condition.** |
| Conjugal partner | 2 | Foreign national in a conjugal relationship with the sponsor for ≥1 year (sponsor is a citizen/PR). Used in family class where marriage/cohabitation not possible. |
| Marriage | 2 | Recognized if valid both under the law of the place where it took place **and** under Canadian law. |
| Restricted occupation | 73 | Occupation the Minister has restricted by numerical limits (for FSW selection). |

**Age "lock-in":** The < 22 threshold is assessed at the **lock-in date** (date the complete application is received), governed by **s.25.1 IRPR** rules on the relevant age-determination date — the child's age is frozen as of that date and a child who turns 22 during processing is not thereby excluded. **[VERIFY exact s.25.1 wording]**. The current age threshold (under 22) applies to applications received **on or after 24 October 2017**; prior threshold was under 19.

**Bad faith relationships (s.4):** A spouse, CLP, conjugal partner or adopted child is **not** a "family member" if the relationship **(a) was entered into primarily for the purpose of acquiring a status or privilege under the Act, or (b) is not genuine** (the s.4 "dual intention/genuineness" test). s.4(2) addresses non-genuine adoptions; **s.4.1** bars a *new* relationship formed after a prior relationship dissolved to acquire status.

### 2.2 Family Class & sponsorship (Part 7)

**Members of the family class (s.117(1)):** spouse, common-law partner, conjugal partner; **dependent child**; **parent or grandparent**; **orphaned** brother/sister/nephew/niece/grandchild who is under 18 and unmarried; a relative of any age where the sponsor has no other listed relative and no Canadian relative ("lonely Canadian"/last-remaining-relative rule); and a child the sponsor intends to adopt.

**Excluded relationships — s.117(9)** (a sponsored person is **NOT** a member of the family class if):
- **117(9)(a)** — spouse/CLP/conjugal partner who is under 18 (minimum age 18).
- **117(9)(b)** — applicant married the sponsor but a prior marriage of either is still subsisting.
- **117(9)(c)** — the marriage is not genuine / was entered into primarily to acquire status (overlaps s.4); or one party was not physically present at the ceremony (proxy/telephone marriage bar) **[VERIFY current 117(9)(c.1) proxy wording]**.
- **117(9)(d)** — **the foreign national was a non-accompanying family member of the sponsor (or of the sponsor's spouse/CLP) at the time the sponsor previously applied to immigrate, and was NOT examined.** This is the high-risk "undisclosed/un-examined dependant" bar — no relief except H&C / public-policy exemptions. Cross-reference **s.125(1)(d)** for the equivalent bar in the Spouse/CLP in Canada Class.

**Multiple-spouse / s.117(9)(b)–(c):** A person cannot be sponsored as a spouse where the sponsor already has a sponsored/married spouse (anti-polygamy).

#### Sponsors (Division 3)

| Requirement | Section | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Who may sponsor | 130(1) | Canadian citizen or PR, **≥18**, **resides in Canada**, files a sponsorship application. |
| Citizen abroad | 130(2) | A citizen not residing in Canada may sponsor a spouse/CLP/conjugal partner or a **dependent child who has no children**, if the sponsor **will reside in Canada** when the FN becomes a PR. PRs **cannot** sponsor from outside Canada. |
| 5-year bar (re-sponsoring a spouse) | 130(3) | A sponsor who was themselves sponsored as a spouse/CLP/conjugal partner cannot sponsor a new spouse/partner **unless they have been a PR/citizen for ≥5 years**. |
| Undertaking — duration | 132(1)(b) | **Spouse / CLP / conjugal partner: 3 years**; **dependent child < 22: earlier of 10 years or until age 25**; **dependent child ≥ 22: 3 years**; **parent/grandparent: 20 years**; **any other person: 10 years**. (Quebec undertakings differ.) |
| Sponsorship requirements | 133(1) | Sponsor must be a qualified sponsor (s.130); intend to fulfil the undertaking; **not in default** of an existing undertaking or support order; **not receiving social assistance** other than for disability; **not bankrupt** (undischarged); **not subject to a removal order**; and **not convicted** (s.133(1)(e)) of a sexual offence / an indictable violent offence punishable by ≥10 years / an offence causing bodily harm to a family member (subject to record-suspension / rehabilitation exceptions). |
| **Minimum Necessary Income (MNI)** | 133(1)(j) | For most sponsorships the sponsor must show **total income ≥ MNI** = the applicable **Low Income Cut-Off (LICO)** for the relevant family-unit size (Statistics Canada figures, urban). |
| **MNI exemption** | 133(4) | **No MNI requirement** where the sponsored person is a **spouse/CLP/conjugal partner with no dependent children of their own**, or a **dependent child (or that child's child) with no children**. **MNI always applies to parent/grandparent (PGP) sponsorships, and PGP requires LICO + 30% for 3 consecutive tax years** (s.133(1)(j)(i)(B) / s.134). |
| Income calculation | 134 | Income = **Notice of Assessment (Line 15000/Total income)** for the relevant taxation year(s), excluding listed amounts (provincial training allowances, EI other than special benefits, social assistance, etc.). For PGP, average over 3 most-recent tax years. |
| Default | 135 | Default occurs when government makes a social-assistance payment the sponsor undertook to repay; ends when repaid. |

**Spouse/Common-Law Partner in Canada Class (s.123–129):** in-Canada sponsorship where the sponsored spouse/CLP cohabits with the sponsor in Canada; **s.124** requires the FN to be a temporary resident (or implied status) and to have valid status; **s.125** mirrors the s.117(9) exclusions (incl. the (d) non-examined-dependant bar).

### 2.3 Economic Classes — Federal Skilled Worker selection grid (s.75–83)

**FSW minimum requirements (s.75(2)):**
1. **≥1 year continuous full-time (or equivalent part-time) skilled work experience** within the last 10 years in **NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3** (lead statement + a substantial number of main duties);
2. **Language** test results (< 2 years old) meeting the minimum threshold the Minister sets in all four abilities (currently **CLB 7**);
3. **Educational credential** — Canadian credential **or** foreign credential with an **ECA** (< 5 years old).

**Selection grid — pass mark 67/100 (s.76(1)):**

| Factor | Section | Max points |
|---|---|---|
| Education | s.78 | **25** |
| Official language proficiency | s.79 | **28** (24 first language + 4 second) |
| Work experience | s.80 | **15** |
| Age | s.81 | **12** |
| Arranged employment | s.82 | **10** |
| Adaptability | s.83 | **10** |
| **Pass mark (s.76(2))** | — | **67** |

s.76(1)(b) also requires either **proof of settlement funds** or arranged employment. s.77 fixes the date for assessing requirements (lock-in at application).

### 2.5 Express Entry system (application-management regime)

Express Entry is an **electronic application-management system**, not a stand-alone immigration class. It manages PR applications under **FSW, CEC, and FST** (and PNP "enhanced" streams).

- **Legislative authority:** **IRPA s.10.1–10.3**. The Minister issues **Ministerial Instructions (MIs) respecting the Express Entry system** under **IRPA s.10.3(1)** — these govern the pool, the **expression of interest (EOI)**, the **Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS)**, eligibility to be in the pool, ranking, and **rounds of invitations**. All MIs must be **published** (IRPA s.10.3(4)).
- **Invitations to Apply (ITA):** issued under **IRPA s.10.1**; the FN then has a deadline (currently 60 days) to submit an electronic application.
- **CRS (max 1,200 points):** Core human capital **max 500** (or **460** with an accompanying spouse/CLP) + spouse/CLP factors **max 40** + skill transferability **max 100** + additional points **max 600** (PNP nomination = 600; pre-2025 a qualifying arranged-employment / LMIA gave 50/200 — **arranged-employment CRS points were removed in 2025; [VERIFY effective date]**).
- **Category-based selection (IRPA s.10.3(1)(h)):** Minister may issue invitations to candidates with specified attributes (e.g., French-language proficiency, healthcare, trades, STEM, transport, agri-food) to support an economic goal. Reported to Parliament annually.
- The **points grids in the Regulations** (s.78–83) belong to the *FSW eligibility* assessment (67-point pass mark) — **distinct from CRS**, which lives in the MIs, not in IRPR.

### 2.6 Work permits (Part 11)

**Issuance & refusal — s.200:** An officer **shall** issue a work permit if the FN: applied per Division 2; **will leave Canada** when the permit expires (s.200(1)(b) — N/A to certain in-Canada applicants); has met the relevant requirement; and either an **LMIA confirms** the offer (s.200(1)(c)(ii)) **or** the work is **LMIA-exempt** (s.200(1)(c)(i)/(ii.1)). **Refuse** if (s.200(3)): unable to perform the work; engaged in unauthorized study/work in the prior 6 months; **employer on the ineligible-employer list** or non-compliant; missing employer compliance fee; or employment with an employer offering striptease/erotic/escort services.

**LMIA-based stream:**
| Provision | Content |
|---|---|
| **s.203** | Officer (on **ESDC's labour-market assessment / opinion**) confirms the offer: genuineness (s.200(5)), effect on the labour market (jobs/wages/working conditions, labour shortage, recruitment efforts, labour dispute), wage at prevailing levels, transition plan where required. Underpins the **Temporary Foreign Worker Program (TFWP)**. |

**LMIA-exempt streams (International Mobility Program, IMP):**
| Section | Basis | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **s.204** | International agreements | **CUSMA/USMCA** professionals & traders/investors; intra-company transferees under treaties; **GATS**; provincial agreements (e.g., PNP work permits); seasonal agricultural. |
| **s.205** | **Canadian interests** | (a) **significant benefit** to Canada (incl. entrepreneurs, intra-company transferees, francophone mobility); (b) **reciprocal employment** (e.g., IEC / youth exchange, exchange professors); (c) designated by Minister re research/competitiveness; (d) charitable/religious work. |
| **s.206** | No other means of support | Refugee claimants; persons under an unenforceable removal order. **Open work permit.** |
| **s.207** | Applicants in Canada | Members of the **Spouse/CLP in Canada Class**; protected persons; H&C applicants in certain cases. **Open work permit.** |
| **s.207.1** | Vulnerable workers | **Open work permit** for workers experiencing or at risk of **abuse** in the context of employment (employer-specific permit holders). |
| **s.208** | Humanitarian | Destitute students; holders of a TRP valid ≥6 months. |

**Open work permits** (no employer/LMIA tie) arise chiefly under **s.205 (some), s.206, s.207, s.207.1**, plus **spousal OWPs** and the **Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)**.

**PGWP** is issued as an LMIA-exempt open work permit (administered under **s.205(c)(2) / LMIA-exemption code C43**, public-policy based — **[VERIFY exemption code]**), for graduates of an eligible program at a **PGWP-eligible DLI**; duration tied to program length (up to 3 years). **2024–2025 field-of-study and language rules now apply — see §3.**

**Employer compliance & AMPs (s.209.1–209.997):** employer conditions (genuine offer, same employment, record-keeping, no reprisal); inspections; **Administrative Monetary Penalties** classified by points (Schedule 2), penalty amounts and ineligibility periods; public list of non-compliant employers (s.209.997).

### 2.9 Inadmissibility, removal, detention regs

**Inadmissibility (Part 3, s.14–24.5):** procedural rules supporting **IRPA s.34–42** (security, human/international rights violations, **serious criminality s.36(1)/criminality s.36(2)**, organized crime, health s.38, financial s.39, misrepresentation s.40, non-compliance s.41, inadmissible family member s.42). Regs prescribe:
- **s.17–18** — equivalency / classification of foreign offences.
- **s.20** — assessment of inadmissibility on entry.
- **Rehabilitation:** deemed rehabilitation timelines and applications for rehabilitation (cross-ref fee s.309).
- **s.24.1+** — ministerial **declaration of relief** procedure.

**Removal orders (Part 13, s.223–243.1):**
- Three classes: **departure order (s.224)**, **exclusion order (s.225)**, **deportation order (s.226)**.
- **s.224** — a departure order is **enforced** if the FN leaves and certifies departure within **30 days**; failure → automatically becomes a **deportation order**.
- **s.225** — exclusion order: **1-year bar** on return (or **5 years** for misrepresentation) without an **Authorization to Return to Canada (ARC)**; deportation = **indefinite** ARC requirement.
- **s.230** — Minister may impose an **administrative deferral / stay** of removals to certain countries; **s.231–234** stays pending judicial review, appeal, PRRA.
- **s.238–243** — voluntary departure / who pays removal costs / when an order is enforced.

**Detention & release (Part 14, s.244–250):**
- **s.244** — factors: whether the person is a **flight risk** (s.245), a **danger to the public** (s.246), or whether **identity** has not been established (s.247).
- **s.248** — other factors before detention/continued detention: reason for detention, length, anticipated future length, unexplained delays, **alternatives to detention**, **best interests of a directly-affected child**.
- **s.249–250** — special considerations for **minors** (detention as a measure of last resort) and the s.45 deposit/guarantee regime.
