# Canadian Immigration — Legal Framework Reference (bar/RCIC-exam grade)

**Current to May 30, 2026.** This is the "hard law" layer beneath the program content — statute-cited reference so every page is authoritative and our knowledge is provably exam-grade. Statutes: **IRPA** (Immigration & Refugee Protection Act, SC 2001 c.27) and **IRPR** (Regulations, SOR/2002-227), on laws-lois.justice.gc.ca.

> **Honesty / compliance disclaimer:** This is reference material, **not legal advice**. Several figures (fees, thresholds) re-index annually and several 2026 items are in flux — every **[VERIFY]** must be checked against the live official page before publishing. Paid immigration advice in Canada may only be given by a **lawyer, Quebec notary, or RCIC** (IRPA s.91). **Refugee claims, PRRA, RAD and IRB representation require an RCIC-IRB or lawyer** — for a standard RCIC consultancy these are *educational/referral* topics, not services.

## 2. CRS — full point tables (reference-grade)

Legal basis: **Ministerial Instructions under IRPA s.10.3** (administrative — changes without regulatory amendment). Maxes: **Core 500 (w/ spouse) / 460 (no spouse) · Spouse 40 · Skill transferability 100 · Additional 600 · TOTAL 1,200.**

> **⚠ 2025 CHANGE:** Arranged-employment / LMIA job-offer points (was +50/+200) **REMOVED effective Mar 25 2025** — gone from all tables. A 2026 consultation may reintroduce limited high-wage-offer points — **not in force** [VERIFY].

**Age** (max 100 w/spouse · 110 no-spouse): 20–29 = 100/110; 30 = 95/105; 35 = 70/77; 40 = 45/50; 44 = 5/6; 45+ = 0.
**Education** (max 140/150): secondary 28/30 · 1-yr 84/90 · bachelor's/3-yr 112/120 · two+ creds 119/128 · master's 126/135 · **PhD 140/150**.
**First official language** per ability (max 32/34 each, 128/136 total): CLB7 16/17 · CLB8 22/23 · CLB9 29/31 · CLB10+ 32/34.
**Second official language** per ability (max 22/24): CLB5–6 = 1 · CLB7–8 = 3 · CLB9+ = 6.
**Canadian work experience** (max 70/80): 1yr 35/40 · 3yr 56/64 · 5+yr 70/80.
**Spouse factors** (max 40): education max 10 · first language max 20 (per ability: CLB7–8 = 3, CLB9+ = 5) · Canadian experience max 10.
**Skill transferability** (max 100; each sub-group capped 50): education × (language CLB9+ or 2yr Cdn exp) → up to 50; foreign experience × (language CLB9+ or 2yr Cdn exp) → up to 50; trade certificate × language → up to 50.
**Additional (max 600):** **PNP nomination +600** · French NCLC7+ & low English **+25** / & English CLB5+ **+50** · Canadian study 1–2yr **+15** / 3yr+ **+30** · sibling in Canada **+15**.

## 3. Inadmissibility — IRPA ss.34–42 (the core of "complex case" work)

Standard of proof = **"reasonable grounds to believe"** (s.33), except "committing an act" limbs (balance of probabilities).

| § | Ground | Key thresholds |
|---|---|---|
| 34 | Security (espionage, subversion, terrorism, **membership**) | PR+FN; A42.1 ministerial relief available |
| 35 | Human/international rights violations | only 35(1)(b) eligible for A42.1 relief |
| **36(1)** | **Serious criminality** | Cdn conviction max **≥10 yrs** OR **>6 months imposed**; OR foreign offence w/ Cdn-equivalent max ≥10 yrs. Applies to **PR**. **No IAD appeal if ≥6 mo imposed (s.64)** |
| **36(2)** | Criminality | Cdn indictable / two summary; foreign equivalent. **FN only** |
| 36(3)(a) | Interpretation | **Hybrid offences = indictable** (the key equivalency rule) |
| 37 | Organized criminality (membership; people smuggling, trafficking, laundering) | A42.1 relief available |
| **38** | Health | (a) public health danger (b) public safety (c) **excessive demand**. **2026 threshold ≈ $28,878/yr ($144,390/5yr)** [VERIFY]. **38(2) exemptions:** sponsored spouses/children, refugees |
| 39 | Financial | unable/unwilling to support self |
| **40** | **Misrepresentation** | material fact/withholding that "could induce error"; **5-year bar (40(2))**; no PR application during bar (40(3)) |
| 41 | Non-compliance (overstay, unauthorized work, **PR residency-obligation breach s.28**) | catch-all |
| 42 | Inadmissible family member | FN only; 42(2) shields protected persons / prescribed TR classes |
| 42.1 | Ministerial relief (34, 35(1)(b), 37) | "national interest"; only security/safety considered |

**Equivalency analysis (foreign convictions):** match essential elements to nearest *Criminal Code* offence → apply hybrid=indictable → read **Canadian** maximum → classify 36(1) vs 36(2).

### Remedies
- **Deemed rehabilitation** (automatic, no fee, IRPR s.18): **10 yrs** since sentence complete for one indictable (Cdn max <10 yr); **5 yrs** for two+ summary. **Unavailable for serious criminality** (max ≥10 yr).
- **Individual criminal rehabilitation** (IRPR s.17): **5 yrs** after *entire* sentence complete (incl. probation/fines); permanent fix; **foreign convictions only**; fees **$246.25 (non-serious) / $1,231 (serious)** [VERIFY].
- **TRP** (IRPA s.24): enter/stay despite inadmissibility; need-vs-risk test; valid up to 3 yrs (work/study only if ≥6 mo); fee **$239.75** [VERIFY].
- **ARC** (IRPR s.52): departure order complied = none; **exclusion = 1 yr (5 yr if misrep)**; **deportation = always**; fee **$400** + removal-cost reimbursement.
- **Record suspension (Parole Board)** for **Canadian** convictions (s.36(3)(b) bars IRCC reliance); **foreign pardons not auto-recognized**.
- **DUI (Bill C-46, Dec 18 2018):** max raised to 10 yrs → now **serious criminality**, no deemed rehab post-2018 (pre-2018 may be grandfathered). High-volume US-visitor topic.

## 4. Status maintenance, restoration, removals, detention, stays

- **Maintained status** (IRPR s.183(5); ex-"implied status"): apply to extend **before** expiry + stay in Canada → keep working/studying **under same conditions** until decision. **Leaving Canada forfeits** work/study; can't start a new employer/permit type on it.
- **Restoration** (IRPR s.182): **90-day** window after losing status; must meet original eligibility + no other breaches; **cannot work/study while pending**; pay restoration + permit fee within the 90 days.
- **Removal orders** (IRPR ss.223–226): **Departure** (leave + confirm within 30 days → else auto-converts to **Deportation**) · **Exclusion** (1-yr bar; **5-yr if misrep**) · **Deportation** (permanent, ARC always). Enforceability s.49.
- **s.44 report → Minister's delegate** may refer to ID or make order directly (IRPR s.228). Make **written submissions before** finalization.
- **Detention** (IRPA s.55/58): grounds = danger, flight risk, identity, Minister investigating. **Reviews: 48 hrs → 7 days → every 30 days** (s.57). Alternatives to detention (bond, guarantor, reporting, electronic monitoring) must be considered; minors = last resort.
- **Stays** (IRPR ss.230–234): automatic on RAD JR (s.231), first PRRA (s.232), approved H&C (s.233), TSR countries (s.230) — each with serious-criminality/security exceptions. **Federal Court stay motion** = tripartite test (serious issue, irreparable harm, balance of convenience — Toth/RJR-MacDonald).

## 5. The IRB & appeals

**Four divisions:** **ID** (admissibility hearings, detention reviews) · **IAD** (sponsorship/residency/removal appeals) · **RPD** (refugee claims) · **RAD** (refugee appeals).

| IAD appeal | Deadline | Key |
|---|---|---|
| **Sponsorship** (refused family-class) | **30 days** | **de novo** (new evidence ok); s.67(1)(c) **H&C** + best-interests-of-child; **s.65 H&C only after** family-class membership confirmed; common grounds: bad-faith relationship (IRPR s.4), excluded member (s.117(9)(d)) |
| **Residency obligation** (PR failed 730 days) | **60 days** (decided abroad/PRTD) / **30 days** (inside/POE) | s.63(4); **H&C s.67(1)(c) can save status** even if days not met |
| **Removal order** (PR/protected person) | **30 days** | IAD can **stay** on conditions (s.68); stay auto-cancels on new s.36(1) conviction (s.68(4)) |

**THE s.64 BAR:** **no IAD appeal at all** for security, human-rights, sanctions, **serious criminality (≥6 months imposed)**, or organized criminality.

**RAD:** appeal RPD within **15 days** (notice) / 30 days (perfect record); paper-based; **new evidence only if arose after rejection / not reasonably available** (s.110(4)); correctness standard (Huruglica). **Barred from RAD** (→ Federal Court only): no-credible-basis, manifestly unfounded, STCA-exception claimants, DFNs.

## 6. Refusals & Federal Court (premium-conversion territory)

> **CRITICAL must-state:** **Refused study permits, work permits, visitor visas (TRVs) and eTAs have NO appeal right.** Recourse = **reapply / reconsideration / Federal Court JR** only.

- **Reconsideration** (to IRCC): discretionary, same decision-maker, **no new evidence**, for clear error/overlooked-on-file evidence/unconsidered PFL. **Does NOT pause the JR deadline** — diarize JR protectively.
- **Procedural Fairness Letter (PFL):** not a refusal — the right to respond (typically 7–30 days; medical excessive-demand = 90 days). Answer **point-by-point with documents**; non-response ≈ refusal.
- **GCMS notes / ATIP:** the real reasons; Access/Privacy Act; **$5**; ~30-day response; outside-Canada needs rep + **IMM 5744**. **Since Jul 29 2025 IRCC proactively includes officer decision notes (ODNs)** with many TRV/study/work refusals — check the letter before ordering ATIP.
- **Federal Court judicial review (IRPA ss.72–75):** **leave + JR** two-step; deadlines **15 days (in-Canada) / 60 days (outside)**; standard = **reasonableness** (*Vavilov* 2019 SCC 65; refined *Mason* 2023 SCC 21); remedy = **redetermination, not a visa**; **no appeal to FCA without a certified "serious question of general importance"** (s.74(d)).

## 7. Refugee / H&C / PRRA (mostly educational/referral for an RCIC consultancy)

> **⚠ Bill C-12 (*Strengthening Canada's Immigration System and Borders Act*) — Royal Assent Mar 26 2026, now law.** Adds two asylum **ineligibility** rules (1-year rule; 14-day internal-border rule), applied **retroactively to claims on/after Jun 3 2025**; affected persons keep **PRRA** only. Regulations still being finalized [VERIFY].

- **Refugee:** Convention refugee (**s.96**, nexus to a Convention ground) vs person in need of protection (**s.97**, torture/risk to life/CUTP, no nexus needed). Eligibility screen (s.100–101) → RPD. **STCA** (claim in first safe country): **2023 expansion to the ENTIRE land border**; **14-day rule**; four exception categories (family member in Canada, unaccompanied minor, document holder, public interest).
- **H&C (s.25):** discretionary PR/exemption; test = *Kanthasamy* 2015 SCC 61 ("excite in a reasonable person a desire to relieve misfortune"); factors = **establishment, family ties, best interests of the child (BIOC)**, hardship, health. **Cannot weigh s.96/97 risk (s.25(1.3))**. Bars: no concurrent refugee claim; one H&C at a time; **12-month bar** after failed refugee claim (exceptions: BIOC; risk to life from inadequate medical care). **This is the one genuine RCIC service line here.**
- **PRRA (ss.112–113):** risk assessment before removal; failed claimants limited to **new evidence (s.113(a))**; **12-month bar**; restricted PRRA (serious criminality/security) → s.97 only, yields a stay not status. *(36-month DCO bar is on the books but struck/suspended since 2019 — don't present as operative.)*

## 8. Professional conduct, representation & website compliance (CICC Code, SOR/2022-128)

**Who may be paid reps (IRPA s.91):** lawyers/paralegals, **RCIC/RCIC-IRB (CICC)**, Quebec notaries. Anyone else paid = illegal **ghost consultant** (IRCC won't deal with them; client risks misrep/refusal). **IMM 5476** = full representation (communicate + act); **IMM 5475** = information release only (no authority to act).

**Code key duties:** integrity/honesty; **competence & diligence**; confidentiality (indefinite); conflict-of-interest disclosure + written consent; **written consultation + service agreements** (scope, fees, timelines, College's role); **fees fair & reasonable, advances held in trust**; prompt notice of errors; return client docs within **30 days**; proper withdrawal.

**Marketing prohibitions (Code s.44(2)) — load-bearing for the website:** must NOT (a) make **false/misleading/inaccurate** representations; (b) **guarantee success** of any application; (c) **imply a relationship with the Government of Canada / a province**. Testimonials must be written + approved (s.45).
