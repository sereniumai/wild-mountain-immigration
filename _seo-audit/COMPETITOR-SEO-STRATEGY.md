# Competitor SEO Strategy — How Wild Mountain Immigration Outranks the Giants

**Date:** 2026-05-30
**Competitors analyzed:** canadavisa.com (Cohen Immigration Law), canadim.com (Canadim Law Firm)
**Our position:** Cochrane / Alberta RCIC consultancy (Nicola Wightman, RCIC R706497), DA 23, recovering from ~96% traffic collapse, historical 30k visits/month.

---

## TL;DR — The Winning Wedge

Both giants are **national law firms with huge domain authority but structural blind spots**. We do NOT fight them head-on for "Express Entry," "CRS calculator," or "PNP" (unwinnable — their DA, tools, and CanadaVisa's 10.2M-message forum are entrenched). We **flank them** on four fronts where the best answer is *local, current, specific, and human*:

1. **LOCAL / GEO intent** — they have ZERO Alberta/Cochrane/Calgary/Bow Valley presence.
2. **FRESHNESS on Alberta/AAIP** — both have stale provincial pages (canadavisa AAIP = Apr 2025; canadim Alberta = lists streams that closed in **2018**). The 2026 AAIP EOI overhaul is uncovered by both.
3. **COMPLEX / REFUSAL cases** — fragmented SERP neither giant owns (we position on reapplication/PFL response/GCMS-notes/stronger resubmission — NOT litigation or IRB representation).
4. **PAGE-LEVEL E-E-A-T + SCHEMA** — both use faceless corporate bylines and have NO FAQ schema. A named, licensed, reviewed local RCIC + full structured data wins rich results they leave on the table.

## 2. Where They're Both Weak (Our Attack Surface)

1. **No local geographic depth** — neither ranks for "immigrate to Alberta," "move to Calgary," "Cochrane immigration consultant." SERP won by small niche/provincial sites.
2. **Stale provincial/AAIP content** — recurring, exploitable. Keep our Alberta pages dated + current and we win on relevance + QDF perpetually.
3. **Missing AAIP streams** — canadavisa omits Tourism & Hospitality, Dedicated Healthcare Pathway, Law Enforcement Pathway entirely. canadim's Alberta streams are 8 years out of date.
4. **No FAQ/HowTo schema** — both leave featured snippets & PAA boxes on the table.
5. **No page-level E-E-A-T** — no named authors, bios, license #s, reviews, or case studies on content.
6. **Thin/generic complex-case content** — refusals, PFL responses, reapplication = fragmented SERP, no incumbent.
7. **Friction in tools/funnels** — canadim's CRS tool is email-gated; both run long high-friction assessment forms with no instant booking.
8. **RCIC-vs-lawyer mismatch** — both are law firms; consultant-shoppers seeking a local RCIC get a mismatched result.

Every page we publish must out-execute the giants on the exact axes where they're weak:

4. **LocalBusiness schema** on service/contact pages + consistent NAP + Google Business Profile.
5. **Real depth on the specific long-tail** — out-depth their 40–80 word stream summaries with definitive 1,500–2,500 word pages.
6. **Ungated, low-friction tools + instant booking** — beat email-gated calculators and 8–10 step forms.
7. **Case studies / outcomes / testimonials** — page-level E-E-A-T they structurally lack.
8. **Internal linking discipline** — copy their #1 strength: tight hub→spoke→tool cross-linking.

## 5. Guardrails (don't waste effort)

- **Do NOT chase:** "Express Entry," "CRS calculator," "PNP," "Canada immigration" (broad head terms — their DA/forum/authority hubs are entrenched).
- **Do NOT do Quebec immigration** (business decision — excluded entirely).
