## 2. Practice-Critical Sections in Detail

### 2.1 Objectives and Application — s. 3; Act/Regulations relationship

- **s. 3(1) — Immigration objectives:** economic/social/cultural benefit; enrich the social and cultural fabric while respecting the **bilingual and multicultural character** of Canada; support official-language minority communities; **family reunification**; successful integration with shared rights/responsibilities; program integrity through fair and efficient procedures; facilitate entry of visitors, students and temporary workers; **protect public health and safety / national security**; promote international justice and security.
- **s. 3(2) — Refugee objectives:** the refugee program is "in the first instance about saving lives and offering protection"; fulfil **international obligations**; fair consideration of claimants; offer safe haven to those with a well-founded fear of persecution (race, religion, nationality, political opinion, particular social group) or risk of torture/cruel treatment; deny access to those who are security risks or serious criminals.
- **s. 3(3) — Application/construction:** the Act is to be construed and applied so as to advance Canada's interests, promote accountability and transparency, ensure decisions are consistent with the **Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms**, and comply with **international human rights instruments** to which Canada is signatory.
- **Act/Regs relationship:** IRPA is framework legislation; operational detail lives in the **Immigration and Refugee Protection Regulations (IRPR), SOR/2002-227**. Most enabling provisions end with a "Regulations" section (e.g., 14, 17, 26, 43, 53, 61, 116). Ministerial instructions (e.g., 10.3, 14.1, 87.3) operate below regulations.

### 2.3 Division 2/3 — Foreign Nationals & Permanent Residents; PR Residency Obligation (s. 28)

- **s. 21** — FN becomes a **PR** if the officer is satisfied they applied, met s.20 obligations, and are **not inadmissible**. A **protected person** becomes PR unless inadmissible under **s.34, 35, 35.1, 36(1), 37 or 38**.
- **s. 22** — TR status; **dual intent** expressly permitted (intent to become PR does not preclude TR status if the officer is satisfied the FN will leave by the authorized end date).
- **s. 28 — Residency obligation:**
  - **28(1):** A PR must comply with the obligation **for every five-year period**.
  - **28(2)(a):** Compliance requires the PR to be, on **at least 730 days** in the 5-year period, one of: (i) **physically present in Canada**; (ii) outside Canada **accompanying a Canadian-citizen spouse/common-law partner/parent**; (iii) outside Canada **employed full-time by a Canadian business** or the federal/provincial public service; (iv) outside Canada **accompanying a PR spouse/common-law partner/parent** who is so employed; or (v) prescribed circumstances.
  - **28(2)(c): H&C override** — H&C considerations (including best interests of a child directly affected) can overcome any breach occurring before the determination.

### 2.4 Division 4 — INADMISSIBILITY (ss. 33–42.1)

> **Correct grounds map** (authoritative — secondary summaries frequently mis-number these):

| Section | Ground | Key thresholds / notes |
|---|---|---|
| **33** | Rules of interpretation | Facts include those for which there are **reasonable grounds to believe** they have occurred, are occurring or may occur. |
| **34** | **Security** | Espionage/subversion against a democratic government, terrorism, danger to security, subversion by force, membership in a relevant organization. |
| **35** | **Human or international rights violations** | Crimes against humanity, war crimes, genocide; being a prescribed senior official of a designated regime; sanctions-listed (historically). |
| **35.1** | **Sanctions** | Inadmissibility for persons subject to sanctions (SEMA / JVCFOA "Magnitsky"). |
| **36(1)** | **Serious criminality** | (a) Canadian conviction for an offence punishable by a **max term of ≥ 10 years**, **or** an offence for which a **term of imprisonment of more than 6 months has been imposed**; (b) conviction outside Canada for an act that would be an offence punishable by a max ≥ 10 years in Canada; (c) commission outside Canada of such an act. *Applies to PRs and FNs.* |
| **36(2)** | **Criminality** | (a) Canadian conviction for an **indictable** offence, or **two** summary offences not arising from a single occurrence; (b)/(c) equivalent foreign convictions/acts. *Applies to FNs only.* |
| **36(3)** | Application | Hybrid offences **deemed indictable**; record-suspended/pardoned convictions excluded; **rehabilitation** (deemed or by application after a prescribed period) cures; youth sentences and Contraventions Act matters excluded. |
| **37** | **Organized criminality** | Membership in a criminal organization engaged in a pattern of activity; engaging in transnational crime (people smuggling, trafficking, money laundering). |
| **38** | **Health grounds** | Danger to public health; danger to public safety; reasonably expected to cause **excessive demand** on health or social services (note s.38(2) family-class/refugee/H&C exceptions for excessive demand). |
| **39** | **Financial** | Unable or unwilling to support themselves/dependants and has not satisfied the officer that adequate arrangements (other than social assistance) exist. |
| **40** | **Misrepresentation** | (1) directly or indirectly misrepresenting/withholding material facts. **40(2)(a): inadmissibility lasts 5 years** (from final determination or enforcement of removal) during which the FN remains inadmissible. |
| **40.1** | **Cessation → inadmissibility** | A loss of protected-person status under s.108(2) (cessation) renders the person inadmissible. **[VERIFY exact subsection wording]** |
| **41** | **Non-compliance** | Inadmissible for an act or omission contravening the Act (FN), or for failing to comply with the **s.28 residency obligation** or a condition (PR). |
| **42** | **Inadmissible family member** | An accompanying (or in prescribed cases non-accompanying) family member's inadmissibility renders the FN inadmissible; s.42(2) protected-person exception. |
| **42.1** | **Ministerial relief** | Minister may, on application or own initiative, **declare that ss. 34, 35 or 37 do not apply** to a FN if satisfied it is **not contrary to the national interest** (national security and public safety are the only considerations). **Not** available for ss. 35.1, 36, 38–40. |
| **43** | Regulations | Regulation-making authority for Division 4. |

- **s. 44 — Reports and referral:** (1) an officer of the opinion that a PR or FN in Canada is inadmissible **may prepare a report** to the Minister; (2) the Minister, if the report is well-founded, may refer it to the **Immigration Division** for an admissibility hearing, or in prescribed cases the **Minister's delegate may make a removal order** directly. s.44 is the gateway between an inadmissibility finding and removal proceedings.

### 2.5 Division 5 — Loss of Status & Removal (ss. 44–53)

- **s. 45** — On an admissibility hearing the ID may: recognize a right to enter; authorize entry/PR status; or **make the applicable removal order**.
- **Removal-order types (per IRPR):** **departure order**, **exclusion order**, **deportation order** — differing in re-entry consequences (an unenforced departure order becomes a deportation order; exclusion orders carry a 1-year/5-year (misrep) re-entry bar; deportation orders require an ARC to return).
- **s. 46 — Loss of PR status:** on becoming a citizen; on a final determination of **failure to meet the s.28 residency obligation**; on a final s.45(d) removal order; on a final cessation determination (s.108); etc.
- **s. 48–52** — Enforcement: a removal order is enforceable once in force and not stayed; the FN must leave **immediately**; effect of departure.
- **s. 53** — Regulations.

### 2.6 Division 6 — Detention & Release (ss. 54–61)

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| **54** | ID is the competent body for detention review. |
| **55** | Arrest/detention with or without warrant: identity not established, **flight risk (unlikely to appear)**, **danger to the public**, or DFN status. |
| **56** | Release by officer before first ID review if grounds no longer exist; conditions may be imposed. |
| **57** | ID review timeline: **within 48 hours** of detention (or as soon as possible after), again **within 7 days**, thereafter **at least once every 30 days**. |
| **58** | ID **shall order release** unless satisfied of a statutory ground (danger, flight risk, identity, examination pending, ministerial security opinion); may impose conditions. |
| **58.1** | Conditions / continuation. |
| **60–61** | Minor children detained **only as a last resort**; regulations. |

> The earlier auto-summary that placed the first review at "30 days" reflects only the recurring-review interval; the **48-hour / 7-day / 30-day** cascade in s.57 is the correct sequence.

### 2.7 Division 7 — Right of Appeal (IAD jurisdiction; the s.64 bar; ss. 65–69; stays)

- **s. 63 — IAD jurisdiction:** (1) a **sponsor** may appeal a refusal of a family-class sponsored application; (2) a FN with a permanent resident visa may appeal a removal order made at examination/admissibility hearing; (3) a **PR or protected person** may appeal a removal order; (4) a PR may appeal a **s.28 residency-obligation** determination made abroad; (5) the Minister may appeal an ID decision. **[VERIFY exact subsection assignment of each appeal right]**
- **s. 64 — No appeal (appeal bar):**
  - **64(1):** No appeal to the IAD where the FN/PR (or, for a sponsor, the sponsored FN) has been found inadmissible on grounds of **security, human or international rights violations, sanctions, serious criminality, or organized criminality**. *(verbatim ground list confirmed)*
  - **64(2):** "*serious criminality must be with respect to a crime that was punished in Canada by a term of imprisonment of **at least six months** or that is described in paragraph 36(1)(b) or (c).*" *(verbatim)*
  - **64(3):** No s.63(1) appeal where the decision rests on **misrepresentation**, unless the sponsored FN is the sponsor's **spouse, common-law partner or child**. *(verbatim)*
- **s. 65 — H&C on appeal:** the IAD may consider H&C grounds (with best interests of a child) **only in family-class sponsorship appeals** where the FN is a member of the family class and the sponsor is a sponsor within the meaning of the Regs.
- **s. 67 — Allowing an appeal:** the IAD allows an appeal where, at the time of the decision, **(a)** the decision is **wrong in law or fact or mixed law and fact**; **(b)** a principle of natural justice was breached; or **(c)** (other than a Minister's appeal) **sufficient H&C considerations** warrant special relief, taking into account the best interests of a child directly affected. **[VERIFY verbatim 67(1)(a)–(c)]**
- **s. 68 — Stay of removal order** *(verbatim 68(1)–(4))*:
  - **68(1):** IAD must be satisfied, taking into account the **best interests of a child** directly affected, that **sufficient H&C considerations** warrant special relief in light of all the circumstances.
  - **68(2):** On a stay, the IAD **shall impose any prescribed condition** and may impose any condition it considers necessary; may vary/cancel non-prescribed conditions; may **cancel the stay** and dispose of the appeal.
  - **68(3):** The IAD may **reconsider** the appeal at any time, on application or on its own initiative.
  - **68(4):** Where a PR/FN who was the subject of a stay on a **serious-criminality or organized-criminality** appeal is **convicted of another offence** referred to in s.36(1), the stay is **cancelled by operation of law** and the appeal is terminated.
- **s. 69 — Dismissal;** **s. 71 — Reopening** for breach of natural justice.

### 2.8 Division 8 — Judicial Review (ss. 72–75)

- **s. 72(1):** Judicial review of any matter under the Act is commenced by an **application for leave** to the **Federal Court** (subject to s.86.1). *(verbatim — leave requirement confirmed)*
- **s. 72(2)(b) — Time limits:** the application must be served and filed **within 15 days** (matter arising **in Canada**) or **within 60 days** (matter arising **outside Canada**) after the applicant is notified of or becomes aware of the matter (extendable by a judge for special reasons). *(verbatim)*
- **s. 73:** Minister may apply for judicial review. **[VERIFY]**
- **s. 74 — Procedure** *(verbatim)*: (a) the judge who grants leave fixes the hearing date/place; (b) the hearing is **no sooner than 30 and no later than 90 days** after leave, unless parties agree; (c) the judge disposes of the application **without delay and in a summary way**; (d) an **appeal to the Federal Court of Appeal lies only if the judge certifies a serious question of general importance** and states the question.
- **s. 75:** Regulations.

### 2.10 s. 24 TRP, s. 25 H&C, s. 25.1 / 25.2

- **s. 24 — Temporary Resident Permit:** an officer may issue a TRP to an inadmissible FN or one who does not meet requirements where the officer is of the opinion it is **justified in the circumstances** (subject to ministerial instructions); cancellable at any time. **24(4):** failed refugee claimant cannot request a TRP for **12 months** after rejection (subject to exceptions). **24(5):** DFNs barred for **5 years**.
- **s. 25 — H&C (on request, FN in Canada):** the Minister **must examine** the circumstances of an FN **in Canada** who applies for PR status and is inadmissible (other than under **34, 35, 35.1, 37**) or non-compliant, and **may grant** PR status or an exemption if justified by H&C considerations, **taking into account the best interests of a child** directly affected.
  - **25(1.2) bars on H&C:** another H&C request pending; **pending refugee claim**; **fewer than 12 months** since a refugee claim was rejected/withdrawn/abandoned (subject to risk-to-life-from-inadequate-health-care or best-interests-of-child exceptions); DFN within 5 years.
  - **25(1.3):** in an H&C assessment the Minister may **not** consider the s.96/97 risk factors as such, but **must consider hardship**.
- **s. 25.1 — H&C on the Minister's own initiative.** **s. 25.2 — public policy** grants.

### 2.11 Part 2 — Refugee Protection (ss. 95–116)

| Section | Content |
|---|---|

| **116** | Regulations. |

### 2.12 s. 91 — Representation or advice for consideration (who may represent for a fee)

- **91(1):** No person shall **knowingly, directly or indirectly, represent or advise a person for consideration** — or offer to do so — in connection with a proceeding or application under the Act.
- **91(2) — authorized persons:** (a) a **lawyer** who is a member in good standing of a provincial law society, or a **notary** of the **Chambre des notaires du Québec**; (b) any other **member in good standing of a law society** (e.g., **paralegals** in good standing); (c) a member in good standing of the **College of Immigration and Citizenship Consultants** (the regulated body for RCICs / RISIAs).
- **91(3):** the s.91(1) prohibition does not apply to a **student-at-law** acting under the supervision of a person described in 91(2)(a).
- **91(4):** an **entity** (and its members/employees) acting under an **agreement or arrangement with His Majesty in right of Canada** does not contravene 91(1).
- **Companion offence:** unauthorized representation is an **offence under Part 3** (see s.91 → enforcement; s.126/128 counselling/misrep context). Free advice (no consideration) is not captured by s.91(1).

## 3. Recent Amendments a Practitioner Must Know (2024–2026)

### Bill C-12 — *Strengthening Canada's Immigration System and Borders Act* (44th–45th Parl.; royal assent **March 26, 2026**)

> **Status:** Received **Royal Assent on or about March 26, 2026** (one source cites March 28; IRCC's own news release confirms the measures "have become law" in March 2026). Several measures came into force on assent; the **asylum-eligibility** rules apply to **claims made on or after June 3, 2025** (see below). **[VERIFY exact CIF date and which provisions await Order in Council.]**

Key IRPA changes:

1. **New asylum-eligibility bars (Part 2 / s.100–101 referral rules):**
   - **One-year claim bar:** an asylum claim made **more than one year after the claimant's first entry to Canada on/after June 24, 2020** is **not referred to the IRB** — regardless of departures and re-entries. Applies to claims made **on or after June 3, 2025** (and reaches pending claims per some reporting). **[VERIFY application to already-pending claims.]**
   - **14-day land-border bar:** claims by persons who entered **between ports of entry along the Canada–US land border** and who claim **more than 14 days** after entry are **not referred to the IRB**.
   - Barred claimants are channelled to a **PRRA-type assessment** rather than an RPD hearing. **Unaccompanied minors are exempt.** **[VERIFY statutory mechanism — likely amendments to s.101 and the PRRA scheme.]**

2. **Elimination of the Designated Countries of Origin (DCO) regime** — the s.109.1 DCO framework is repealed. **[VERIFY whether repealed by C-12 or already inoperative from prior litigation/regulatory action.]**

3. **Broad executive document/application powers (s.87.31 and related):** the **Governor in Council** (and Minister) may **cease accepting, suspend, or terminate** the processing of applications, and **cancel, suspend or vary** documents (work permits, study permits, TRVs, **PR visas**, eTAs) **for groups/classes** — in some cases **without individual notice or an opportunity to respond**. A significant new discretionary lever affecting clients with pending applications and valid documents. **[VERIFY exact section numbers and safeguards.]**

4. **New / expanded FN examination and document-production obligations (Division 3.1, s.32.1):** standalone power to compel FNs to **appear for examination** and **produce documents** on demand, with limited procedural protection. **[VERIFY section text.]**

5. **Minister's power to specify required information/documents for refugee claims** (Part 2) — codifies authority to dictate supporting-document requirements for claims. **[VERIFY section.]**

6. **Expanded information-sharing** within IRCC and with federal/provincial/territorial partners under written agreements and stated privacy safeguards.

7. **s. 25 / s. 25.1 interaction:** reporting indicates that, alongside the new asylum bars, affected persons may seek a **deferral of removal** and a **ministerial exemption from the PRRA bar** on **H&C grounds under s.25.1**. **[VERIFY — confirm whether C-12 amended s.25/25.1 text or merely relies on existing discretion.]**

8. **Border-security / enforcement measures** (transnational organized crime, fentanyl, illicit financing) extending beyond IRPA into other statutes. **[VERIFY IRPA-specific provisions.]**
